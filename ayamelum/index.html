Overview

Ayamelum is a Local Government Area in Anambra North Senatorial District. Renowned as an agricultural powerhouse, it utilizes the fertile soil of the Lower Anambra River Basin to produce massive quantities of rice, yams, and cassava.

The headquarters, Anaku, along with towns like Omor, are critical to the food security of southeastern Nigeria. The region is also rich in aquatic life, making fishing a secondary economic pillar.

Cultural Heritage

The traditions of the Omambala people are vibrant and deeply connected to the land.

Iwa Ji (New Yam)

A massive celebration across all 8 communities, marking the harvest season. The yam is king in Ayamelum.

Fishing Festivals

Communal fishing in the lakes and tributaries of the Omambala river, often accompanied by contests and feasting.

Mmanwu Festivals

Ayamelum is known for powerful masquerades that perform during the dry season and festive periods.

Destinations & Landmarks

Lower Anambra Irrigation Project (Omor)

Witness the scale of the famous Omor Rice. This massive irrigation project supports year-round farming and is a sight to behold for anyone interested in agriculture and engineering.

Agro-Tourism

The Omambala River

The lifeblood of Ayamelum. It offers scenic boat rides, fresh fish markets at the banks, and a cool breeze that defines the local climate.
