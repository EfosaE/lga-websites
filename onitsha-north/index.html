About Onitsha North

Location & Geography

Region: Southern Anambra State

Landmass: Approximately 20 square kilometers

Population: Over 200,000 (estimated)

Created: 1991

Onitsha North is strategically located along the eastern bank of the River Niger, serving as a major commercial gateway in southeastern Nigeria. The LGA is home to the famous Onitsha Main Market, one of the largest markets in West Africa, making it the epicenter of trade and commerce in the region.

Historical Significance

Onitsha has been a trading center for centuries, dating back to pre-colonial times when it served as a major market for neighboring communities. The town gained prominence during the colonial era as a river port and has evolved into Nigeria's foremost commercial hub.

The establishment of Onitsha North as a separate local government area in 1991 recognized its unique commercial character and growing importance in regional trade.

Today, Onitsha North is synonymous with enterprise, innovation, and economic dynamism, attracting traders and investors from across Africa and beyond.
