About Onitsha North LGA

Onitsha North is one of the urban local government areas within the Onitsha metropolis of Anambra State, Nigeria. It covers part of the historic river port city of Onitsha, long known as a major centre of trade, transport, faith and culture in the south‑east.

The LGA hosts markets, churches, schools, health facilities, public institutions and dense residential neighbourhoods. It works alongside other Onitsha‑area LGAs and Anambra State agencies on issues such as traffic management, sanitation, public order and infrastructure maintenance.

Administrative Structure

Executive

Headed by the Chairman / Mayor (and where applicable, a Transition Committee), supported by Supervisors and heads of departments.

Legislative (Councillors)

Ward Councillors form the legislative arm at the local level under the Anambra State legal framework and enact bye‑laws for local governance.

Traditional & Community Stakeholders

Traditional institutions, market leaders, religious bodies and resident associations engage with the LGA on community issues, security, cleanliness and social welfare.
