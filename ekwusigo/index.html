Our Roots

A Legacy of Brotherhood

The story of Ekwusigo is one of shared ancestry and resilience. Our name itself is a call to unity. Historically, towns like Oraifite and Ichi trace their lineage to the **Anaedo** clan, sharing a common bond with Nnewi.

Ozubulu, our headquarters, stands as the administrative center, while Ihembosi and Ichi complete the four pillars of our LGA. We are a people who value the land, celebrating the **Uzoiyi** and **New Yam** festivals to honor our harvest.

Economic & Investment Profile

Ekwusigo is strategically positioned between the commercial city of Onitsha and the industrial hub of Nnewi.

Agriculture

Major producer of Yam, Cassava, and Maize. The fertile lands of Ozubulu support large-scale farming.

Agro-Processing Opportunity

Manufacturing

Oraifite is home to significant manufacturing and fabrication industries, benefiting from the Nnewi industrial spillover.

Commerce

Cultural Life

Uzoiyi Festival

"When the masquerades come out in Ubulu, the whole town stops to watch."

Celebrated annually, Uzoiyi is a major cultural event in Ekwusigo, attracting tourists and sons of the soil from abroad.

Spirit of Philanthropy

"We rise by lifting others."

Ekwusigo is known for its influential philanthropists, such as Sir Emeka Offor (from Oraifite), who drive community development through foundations.
