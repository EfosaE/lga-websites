Nnewi North LGA – Data Snapshot

Data‑centric overview based on publicly available information (e.g. Nnewi North article on Wikipedia). Detailed indicators can be connected to live databases or APIs.

Nnewi North is an urban‑leaning Local Government Area in Anambra State, Nigeria. It is centred on Nnewi town, which is traditionally divided into four quarters: Otolo, Uruagu, Umudim and Nnewichi. The area is widely known as a major indigenous industrial and commercial hub.

Education & Health Indicators

Education

Nnewi North hosts a number of notable secondary schools and training institutions. Wikipedia lists schools such as:

  • Girls’ Secondary School, Nnewi
  • Maria Regina Model Comprehensive Secondary School, Nnewi
  • Nnewi High School, Nnewi
  • Nigerian Science & Technical College, Nnewi
  • Okongwu Memorial Grammar School, Nnewi
  • Community/Women education centres and model schools in the quarters

Health

The LGA is home to the Nnamdi Azikiwe University Teaching Hospital (NAUTH) in Nnewi, which provides tertiary medical services and training. There are also multiple private clinics and smaller health facilities across the quarters.

Agricultural Data

Although Nnewi North is heavily urbanized, pockets of agriculture and peri‑urban farming still exist around the fringes and undeveloped land:

  • Root crops: cassava, yam and cocoyam (small‑scale plots).
  • Vegetables: okro, pumpkin leaves and other greens cultivated near homesteads.
  • Poultry and livestock: backyard and small commercial operations.

Commercial Activity Index

Nnewi is widely described as a major manufacturing and commercial centre in Nigeria, hosting indigenous companies in auto parts, plastics, electricals and other goods. Firms such as Innoson Vehicle Manufacturing, Ibeto Group and other indigenous manufacturers are associated with the greater Nnewi area.

The Nkwo Nnewi main market is a critical trading hub for auto parts and a wide variety of goods, attracting buyers and sellers from across Nigeria and West Africa.
