About Oyi Local Government

A rich tapestry of culture, commerce, and community in the heart of Anambra State

Strategic Location

Oyi is located in the Anambra North Senatorial Zone, bordered by Ayamelum and Anambra East LGAs. The area benefits from proximity to the River Anambra, providing rich agricultural lands and fishing opportunities.

Historical Background

Created in 1991 during the state creation exercise, Oyi LGA has evolved from a predominantly agrarian area into a vibrant commercial hub. The area has a rich pre-colonial history with the Nteje and Awkuzu kingdoms being major historical centers of trade and cultural exchange.

Vibrant Population

With an estimated population of over 220,000 people, Oyi is home to industrious and culturally-rich Igbo communities. The people are predominantly traders, farmers, and craftsmen known for their entrepreneurial spirit.

Cultural Heritage

Experience the rich traditions, festivals, and cultural practices that define Oyi

Traditional Festivals

  • Iwa Ji (New Yam Festival) - Celebrated annually in August to mark the harvest season
  • Ofala Festival - Traditional ceremony of the Igwe (King) in various communities
  • Onwa Ato Festival - Cultural celebration featuring masquerades and traditional dances
  • Christmas Carnival - Modern cultural celebration blending tradition with contemporary festivities

Traditional Leadership

  • Igwe of Nteje - HRH Igwe Rowland Odegbo, Eze Nteje III
  • Igwe of Awkuzu - Traditional ruler of Awkuzu community
  • Igwe of Umunya - Traditional head of Umunya town
  • Council of Traditional Rulers - Collaborative leadership body

Crafts & Specialties

  • Pottery & Ceramics - Traditional clay pot making and modern ceramics
  • Weaving - Traditional mat weaving and basket making
  • Woodcarving - Intricate designs on masks, sculptures, and furniture
  • Traditional Attire - Production of Akwete cloth and traditional Igbo garments

Economic Activities

Oyi's economy is driven by commerce, agriculture, and entrepreneurship

Dominant Industries

Trading & Commerce

Nteje market is a major commercial hub in Anambra North, attracting traders from neighboring states. The market operates on traditional Igbo market day cycles (Nkwo, Eke, Afor, Oye).

Agriculture & Farming

Rice farming, cassava cultivation, yam production, and vegetable farming are major agricultural activities. The proximity to River Anambra provides irrigation opportunities.

Fishing & Aquaculture

River Anambra supports thriving fishing communities. Modern fish farming and aquaculture ventures are increasingly popular.

Small-Scale Manufacturing

Processing of agricultural products, palm oil production, garri processing, and small-scale industrial activities.
