Brief Overview

Ihiala is a city and the administrative headquarters of the Ihiala Local Government Area in the southern part of Anambra State, Nigeria. The LGA lies in the south‑west of the state and forms part of the wider Igboland region.

Ihiala LGA covers about 300.8 km² and had an estimated population of roughly 430,800 people as of 2022. It includes towns such as Amorka, Azia, Lilu, Okija, Mbosi, Isseke, Orsumoghu, Ubuluisuzor and Uli, and is known for its mix of agrarian communities, busy markets and mission‑owned schools and hospitals.

Location within Anambra State

South‑west of Anambra State. It borders Nnewi LGA to the north‑west, Ekwusigo LGA to the west, parts of Imo State to the north‑east and east, and Delta State to the south.

Landmass

Approximately 300.8 km² (according to published figures for Ihiala Local Government Area).

Historical Context

The communities that make up Ihiala trace their origins to various Igbo ancestral lineages. Over time, migrations, intermarriages, trade and missionary activity shaped the present‑day settlements and institutions in the LGA.

Cultural Identity of Ihiala

Ihiala is rooted in traditional Igbo culture, enriched by strong Christian influences and vibrant community life. Town unions, age grades, and women associations remain central to social organization, conflict resolution, and communal projects.
